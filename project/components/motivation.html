01 • Motivation: The Call to Arms

The Quest for Equality in Modern Seminars

The Why: Our group chose the Social Track because the seminar environment at XJTLU represents a critical micro-society where power dynamics and cultural barriers often silence brilliant minds. We observed that international students frequently experience "Face-Saving" anxiety, where the fear of public failure outweighs the desire for academic contribution. By choosing this track, we aim to leverage AI not as a replacement for human thought, but as a social equalizer—a digital "Hidden Shield" that allows students to iterate privately before proclaiming publicly. Our goal is to dismantle the invisible hierarchy of the classroom, ensuring that the "Round Table" is not just a metaphor for equality, but a functional reality. We believe that true inclusivity in higher education requires tools that respect psychological safety while actively scaffolding participation, transforming the seminar from a high-stakes stage into a collaborative sanctuary where every voice, regardless of linguistic fluency, finds its throne.

At XJTLU, the seminar is the heart of academic discourse. Yet, for many, it is a battlefield of anxiety. Our motivation stems from the Face-Saving Culture and Cognitive Overload that silence international students.

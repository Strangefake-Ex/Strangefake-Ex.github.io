Target Personas

Understanding the silence to build better conversations.

Primary User

Yifei (The "Silent" Student)

Age 19 • XJTLU Year 2 Student • Introverted

Core Goals

Wants to earn participation marks and share brilliant ideas without the anxiety of speaking flawed English in front of 30 peers and the professor.

Frustrations

  • Needs time to translate thoughts from Chinese to English.
  • Fear of being judged by the academic teacher.
Secondary User

Dr. Sarah (The Facilitator)

Age 35 • Language/Academic Teacher • Extroverted

Core Goals

Wants to accurately assess student understanding, encourage equal participation, and foster a lively academic debate rather than a monologue.

Frustrations

  • Asking a question and getting "dead silence" in return.
  • Traditional forums (like Learning Mall) are too slow and rigid.

User Research & Reflection

From data to human-centric insights.

📊 Survey Highlights

Anxiety about English Fluency 85%
Reported "Dead Silence" in Seminars 72%

💬 Interview Reflection

"It's not that I don't have ideas. It's that the 'Round Table' in class feels like a stage, and if my English isn't perfect, I'm afraid to step on it."

Insight: The core issue is Face-Saving Culture and Cognitive Load. Our solution must provide a 'safety net' (Agent) that lowers the stakes of participation.

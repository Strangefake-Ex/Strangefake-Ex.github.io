01 • Motivation: The Call to Arms

The Quest for Equality in Modern Seminars

The Why: Our group chose the Social Track because the seminar environment at XJTLU represents a critical micro-society where power dynamics and cultural barriers often silence brilliant minds. We observed that international students frequently experience "Face-Saving" anxiety, where the fear of public failure outweighs the desire for academic contribution. By choosing this track, we aim to leverage AI not as a replacement for human thought, but as a social equalizer—a digital "Hidden Shield" that allows students to iterate privately before proclaiming publicly. Our goal is to dismantle the invisible hierarchy of the classroom, ensuring that the "Round Table" is not just a metaphor for equality, but a functional reality. We believe that true inclusivity in higher education requires tools that respect psychological safety while actively scaffolding participation, transforming the seminar from a high-stakes stage into a collaborative sanctuary where every voice, regardless of linguistic fluency, finds its throne.

At XJTLU, the seminar is the heart of academic discourse. Yet, for many, it is a battlefield of anxiety. Our motivation stems from the Face-Saving Culture and Cognitive Overload that silence international students.

04 • Personas: Heroes of the Quest
🛡️
Primary Stakeholder • The Silent Knight

Yifei

Brilliant Mind • Year 2 Student • XJTLU

Her Quest

Primary Goal: To find a collaborative atmosphere that mitigates linguistic vulnerability, allowing her to contribute her profound analytical perspectives without fear.

As a bright undergraduate with a cautious mindset about her second-language proficiency, she would benefit tremendously from an AI-assisted platform to privately polish her vocabulary and submit ideas securely.

Her Dragons

  • ›
    Native Speaker Dominance: Highly fluent peers often seize speaking opportunities before she can finish mentally translating her complex sentences.
  • ›
    Vulnerability to Scrutiny: A severe fear of grammatical stumbling or appearing unpolished causes her to withhold superior academic arguments.
  • ›
    Dual Cognitive Burden: The process of live translation consumes her mental bandwidth, often leading to oversimplified or delayed contributions.
👥
Secondary Stakeholder • The Grand Mentor

Dr. Sarah

Facilitator • Visionary • Academic Lead

Her Quest

Primary Goal: To transform her seminars into vibrant intellectual ecosystems where every student feels empowered to contribute to the central dialogue.

She needs an unobtrusive analytical lens to reveal participation patterns and quantify the group's collaborative vitality without disrupting the natural rhythm of teaching.

Her Dragons

  • ›
    The Engagement Illusion: She struggles to differentiate between deep, reflective silence and simple disengagement, causing valuable student insights to remain untapped.
  • ›
    Antiquated Diagnostics: Relying on subjective observation provides no reliable data on participation equity, forcing her to navigate classroom dynamics blindfolded.
  • ›
    The Intellectual Echo Chamber: She fears discussions become a feedback loop for vocal students, creating a monoculture where quieter, dissenting perspectives are never heard.

06 • Requirements: The Code of Chivalry

Functional Requirements

  • ✦ Multi-agent AI scaffolding for real-time thought polishing.
  • ✦ Anonymous 'Safe-Zone' for initial drafting.
  • ✦ Facilitator dashboard with engagement heatmaps.
  • ✦ Real-time speech-to-text with translation support.

Non-Functional Requirements

  • ✦ Latency < 200ms for seamless dialogue flow.
  • ✦ High accessibility (WCAG 2.1 compliant).
  • ✦ Privacy-first data encryption for student thoughts.
  • ✦ Scalability for up to 100 concurrent seminar users.

"Playful" Must-Haves

  • ✦ Private Draft Sanctuary: Students draft privately, get AI polishing help, then choose when to publish to group discussion.
  • ✦ Balanced Participation Monitor: Teacher dashboard shows speaking distribution, silence duration, and underrepresented voices in real time.
  • ✦ Peer-First AI Prompting: AI nudges only when discussion stalls, and prompts peer interaction instead of replacing human dialogue.

Design Alternatives & Selection

Alt A: Pure Text Chat

A standard messaging interface. Rejected because it lacks the "Social" track's focus on egalitarianism and psychological safety.

Alt B: Voice-Only AI

Real-time voice translation. Rejected due to high latency and potential to increase anxiety rather than reduce it.

Selected: Hybrid Scaffolding

Combines private drafting, AI polishing, and anonymous contribution. Chosen because it directly addresses the "Face-Saving" gap identified in research.

08 • Prototype: The Magical Mirror

System Architecture

INPUT LAYER

  • Student client input (draft / publish)
  • Teacher dashboard actions
  • Session metadata (group, topic, role)

STATE & LOGIC LAYER

  • Front-end session state (draft, sent, acknowledged)
  • Prompt-response handler for AI scaffolding
  • Nudge logic (silence threshold / stuck detection)

OUTPUT LAYER

  • Public round-table thread updates
  • Participation analytics aggregation
  • Teacher notifications and equity report

Data Handling Evidence

Each student interaction updates local state and is mirrored to session logs used by analytics cards (talk-time ratio, silent-window, contribution count).

Implementation Scope

Alpha uses front-end mock data plus deterministic rules to simulate AI nudges while preserving the same state transitions planned for production APIs.

V1.0 Low-Fi Insights

Initial testing showed that students felt 'over-monitored' by the AI. We pivoted to a 'Pull' model where the AI only intervenes when requested or during prolonged silence.

V2.0 High-Fi Refinement

Added the 'Sanctuary Mode'—a private drafting space that uses Arthurian metaphors to make the technology feel more like a supportive companion than a rigid tool.

09 • Evaluation & Reflection

TESTER 01 • STUDENT

Task

Draft one discussion point and decide whether to publish it.

Feedback

"Private drafting reduces anxiety, but I need clearer status after publishing."

Change

Added explicit post states: Draft Saved, Sent to Group, Acknowledged.

TESTER 02 • STUDENT

Task

Use AI help to polish text during a silent phase.

Feedback

"AI suggestions are useful, but too frequent prompts feel distracting."

Change

Switched to peer-first nudging: AI activates only after silence threshold.

TESTER 03 • TEACHER

Task

Monitor one group and identify low-participation members.

Feedback

"I can spot silent students faster, but I need actionable intervention hints."

Change

Added intervention hints with suggested neutral transition phrases.

Social & Ethical Reflection

  • Psychological safety is prioritized through private drafting and voluntary publishing.
  • Teacher analytics are used for inclusion support, not punitive ranking.
  • Language diversity is respected by offering scaffolded expression without forcing uniform style.

AI Use Reflection

  • AI is limited to support roles: rephrasing, confidence nudging, and summarizing.
  • Core design logic, user findings, and requirement decisions are authored by the team.
  • Known risk: over-reliance on AI wording; mitigation is peer-first prompting and human validation.
